    How to on everything usability testing

Welcome to our simple guide on how to do a discount usability test, there is a sample one at the bottom of this page but to show you how easy it is to make one, why not follow the simple tutorial below.



First thing you have to is to make out 10 tasks for the user to do on your website, here are a few examples
  • Go and search for a product on this page
  • Go to put an item into the cart
  • Go to contact customer support about a return

When deciding your task, you should make these so they suit what a typical user journey would be like on your website, this is dependant on business goals also.




Next thing to do is to have a list of questions ready to be asked while the user is going through tasks. Not all questions will need to be asked for each task so keeping the list of questions small is key to remembering which ones to ask for each task. Here are some that any website could use:

  • What is your initial impression on the page and would you find it trust worthy and why?
  • Where would you expect to find more information on this item
  • What motivated you to click (specific element) or what would you click next?
  • How long did it take the user to complete a task?

The key to these questions is to observe the participant and watch where there mouse goes on the screen so if you think they look confused or if they are taking time to complete a task then take a note and ask them at the end of the tasks on why they took so long at a certain point. Try to keep the user flowing through the tasks as much as possible as if they were actually browsing your website.
